ANALYSIS OF CATEGORICAL DATA USING R SOFTWARE
Brief Intro:

A workshop on the "Analysis of Categorical Data with R" would be a valuable investment for any business or organization looking to improve their data analysis capabilities. Categorical data is a type of data that is divided into groups or categories rather than being measured on a continuous scale, and it is commonly found in many areas of business, including marketing, finance, and operations.

By learning how to analyze categorical data with R, participants in the workshop would gain a powerful tool for making data-driven decisions and improving business performance. R is a widely used statistical software program that provides a flexible and comprehensive framework for analyzing data, and it is particularly well-suited for handling categorical data.

During the workshop, participants would learn how to use R to analyze categorical data, including techniques such as contingency tables, chi-square tests, and logistic regression. They would also learn how to visualize and interpret their results, enabling them to communicate their findings effectively to stakeholders across the organization.

By investing in a workshop on the analysis of categorical data with R, businesses and organizations can improve their data analysis capabilities, gain valuable insights into their operations, and make more informed decisions. Additionally, the skills and knowledge gained from the workshop can be applied across a wide range of business domains, providing long-term value and return on investment.

Categorical data are by no means restricted to the social and biomedical sciences. These sets of data also frequently occur in the behavioral sciences (e.g., type of mental illness, with the categories schizophrenia, depression, neurosis), epidemiology and public health (e.g., contraceptive method at last sexual intercourse, with the categories none, condom, pill, IUD, other), genetics (type of allele inherited by an offspring), botany and zoology (e.g., whether or not a particular organism is observed in a sampled quadrat), education (e.g., whether a student response to an exam question is correct or incorrect), and marketing (e.g., cons preference among the three leading brands of a product). These datasets even occur in highly quantitative fields such as engineering sciences and industrial quality control. Examples are the classification of items according to whether they conform to certain standards, and subjective evaluation of some characteristic: how soft to the touch a certain fabric is, how good a particular food product tastes, or how easy a worker finds it to perform a certain task. Categorical variables are of many types. In this workshop we shall provide ways of classifying them and analyzing relevant real-life datasets with R software. The two-day physical workshop will showcase many ways of writing R commands to solve problems that are categorical in nature.
